Characterization of audio elements

Audio
Characterization
Anechoic room

Performed in-depth characterization and analysis of audio components to evaluate their performance

Contexte

Who needs the Euro class when curiosity and headphones can take you straight to the sound lab ?

As part of my second year in the BUT Applied Physics and Measurement program, we were required to complete a small projectâ€”either in English for students in the Euro class or in French for others. Although I am not part of the Euro class, I found the topic reserved for Euro students very interesting. Therefore, I requested to work on a Euro project. I worked on project no. 12, the characterization of audio components, for four weeks with three other team members. The characterization of components is a fundamental study in the bachelor of applied physics and measurement engineering. More especially in the acoustic domain, as this department is being charged by private industry to conduct audio components characterization. As the measurement procedure is not fixed, the mission of this project will be to conduct different characterization on audio components in order to establish an adapted protocol for each type of measure. Three different procedures were then conducted: the directivity, the demonstration of the proximity effect and the noise cancellation measurement of different headphones.

To make exprimentation we had an anechoic room, different speakers, microphone and sound level meter. we are reparte the tasks and I work on the group who studing the directivity of a loud speaker. Secondly as we still had time we have can make a study of the noise cancellation measurement of different headphones with a Sony WH-1000XM4 , a Marshall monitor II and other.

My job

During this project, I was responsible for setting up the experiments, conducting them, and acquiring data. We carried out approximately fifty measurements, and I also analyzed the collected data using specialized software

Conclusion

From a general point of view, this project enriched my skills in english and acoustics. It followed our practical work on studying a loudspeaker, offering logical and practical continuity in our learning. I deepened my knowledge and filled in some gaps, particularly by learning how to use specialized software for acoustic data analysis. I also acquired skills in setting up measurement chains, including selecting and calibrating instruments, configuring systems, collecting and analyzing data. These skills are essential for future work in laboratories or industries. We also created infographics to present our results clearly and visually as you can see lower. Teamwork was particularly effective, with each member finding their place and contributing significantly. Finally, this project was successfully completed. It allowed I to apply and develop new skills. I am now better prepared to face future challenges in acoustics and physical measurements.

Point of amelioration

Through this project, I improved my English skills; however, I still have some gaps and recognize the need for further practice to continue improving.
